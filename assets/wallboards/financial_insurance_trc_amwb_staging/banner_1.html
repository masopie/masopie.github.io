Sometimes referred to as a “maximum benefits cap”, this is a cap on the benefits your insurance plan will pay in a calendar year. Caps can be placed on particular services such as medications or hospitalizations. Once the coverage cap has been reached, you are responsible for cost of care provided thereafter, through the end of the year.
This is your share of the cost of healthcare service provided to you. Co-insurance is calculated as a percentage of the amount of a service.
This is the fixed, contracted amount you are required to pay at the time you receive eligible care. Co-pays are due to the healthcare provider at the time of your visit. Co-pay amounts vary depending on your health insurance plan.
This is a manufacturer-sponsored savings that may reduce the out-of-pocket cost for certain medications.
This is your out-of-pocket responsibility for receiving care before your plan coverage takes effect. For example, a $500 deductible is your out-of-pocket share of cost coverage. A deductible will reset beginning each new calendar year of coverage.
This is a summary of that you will receive from your insurance company when they receive a bill for medical services. It will explain the allowable charge, how much they have paid, and the portion that you have to pay.
List of prescription medications covered under the terms of your plan.
The maximum out-of-pocket amount you will pay before the plan you selected will begin to cover 100% of health care services or medication costs.
A monthly amount you pay in order to have health insurance. A premium is typically paid monthly, quarterly, or yearly.
This is approval you must receive from some insurance companies require before they decide if they will pay for certain services, procedures or medications.
